Reducing Time Spent on Low-Impact Work

The Problem:

Managers spend 40% of their time on low-value, non-strategic tasks (e.g., admin, unnecessary meetings, firefighting). With an average manager salary of R600,000 per year, that’s R240,000 per manager per year spent on low-impact work.

Illustrative Example:

A 20% reduction in time wasted frees up R48,000 per manager per year for high-value work.

The Impact:

Across 25 managers, this translates to R1.2 million saved per year in reduced time wasted.

Improving Team Execution to Reduce Oversight

The Problem:

Managers spend 35% of their time overseeing tasks that could be completed independently. At an average salary of R600,000 per year, this equates to R210,000 per manager per year spent on oversight. A 20% improvement in team execution reduces oversight time, saving R42,000 per manager per year.

Illustrative Example:

A 20% improvement in team execution reduces oversight time, saving R42,000 per manager per year.

The Impact:

Across 25 managers, this results in R1 million saved per year in recovered productivity.

Lowering Turnover Through Structured Development

The Problem:

59% of employees don’t have structured career development plans. Employees without clear growth paths are more likely to leave—assuming 10% annual turnover, that’s 15 employees per year. A 25% reduction in turnover risk, meaning 7 employees a year are retained, prevents R1 million per year in replacement costs. Gallup estimates the cost of turnover at 50% of their salary (R150,000 per lost employee).

Illustrative Example:

By implementing structured career development, the number of employees without a plan is reduced to 30%. This results in a 25% reduction in turnover risk, meaning 7 employees a year are retained.

The Impact:

Retaining 7 employees prevents R1 million per year in replacement costs.
